About

Memo-Haus is a living memory archive, held inside a small pavilion at the edge of the city, as a field of moments in light and sound. A photograph becomes a place you can walk into. A voice becomes something you can stand close to and listen to again.

Every memory here is real, and every one of them is equally home.

Anyone can add one: a photo old or new, its year, a story, a voice note or a piece of music if they like. It becomes a three-dimensional scene and takes its place beside everyone else's, on a shared map that keeps growing at the pavilion by the ZOB junction.

Walk through it with your own phone as the guide, or drag the time slider to travel straight to a decade. Come close to a memory and it opens, its story and its sound rising to meet you; step back and it settles into quiet again, one more presence in the field.

It isn't a monument, and it isn't an archive in the usual sense. It's closer to a chorus: hundreds of small, ordinary moments, each one kept exactly as it was given.

Wolfsburg is known worldwide for its industry. Memo-Haus adds a second identity to that, made entirely from the people who call it home.
